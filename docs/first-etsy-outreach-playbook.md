# VitrinY First Etsy Outreach Playbook

This document turns VitrinY from a website into a repeatable sales system.

## Current goal

Get the first 3 paid VitrinY clients by starting with a small, easy-to-understand offer:

- Studio Listing Refresh: $99–$149
- Product Launch Kit: $199–$349
- Commerce Brand Page: $299–$499
- Commerce Catalog System: $699–$1,200+

The first offer should usually be Studio Listing Refresh because it is easier for an Etsy seller to understand than a full website project.

## Ideal first customer profile

Target Etsy sellers who already have product-market activity but weak presentation.

Good signals:

- 50+ sales
- 10+ reviews
- Products are visually sellable but listing images feel inconsistent
- Shop has a clear category: fashion, jewelry, home decor, digital products, invitations, gifts
- Seller uses Instagram, Pinterest, or external traffic links
- Product prices are high enough to care about conversion
- Shop looks like it could become a brand, not only a casual side shop

Avoid at first:

- Very new shops with no sales
- Extremely large professional brands with internal teams
- Shops selling copyrighted or questionable products
- Low-price commodity shops
- Shops with no visual differentiation

## Mini-audit structure

For each target shop, write a 5-point audit before contacting them.

### 1. Brand clarity

Question: Does the shop immediately communicate what it sells and why it is special?

Score:

- 1 = generic shop
- 2 = some identity but unclear
- 3 = clear enough
- 4 = strong brand direction
- 5 = premium, memorable brand

### 2. Listing cover strength

Question: Would the product cover image stop a scroll on Etsy or Pinterest?

Score 1–5.

### 3. Product context

Question: Do the images show scale, use case, lifestyle, detail, and buyer confidence?

Score 1–5.

### 4. External traffic readiness

Question: If traffic comes from Instagram, Pinterest, or Google, is there a strong landing experience?

Score 1–5.

### 5. Best VitrinY offer

Choose one:

- Studio Listing Refresh
- Product Launch Kit
- Commerce Brand Page
- Commerce Catalog System

## If they ask price

The easiest starting point is usually a Studio Listing Refresh.

For one product, it usually starts around $99–$149 depending on the number of visuals needed.

That can include a stronger hero direction, supporting listing image concepts, lifestyle mockup direction, and a short visual structure for the listing.

If the shop needs a full brand page or catalog website later, that would be a separate Commerce package.

## If they are interested in a website

For Etsy sellers, I usually do not recommend jumping straight to Shopify unless they are ready to run a full independent ecommerce operation.

VitrinY Commerce is lighter: it creates a professional brand website and product presentation layer, but purchase buttons still send buyers to your official Etsy listings.

That keeps Etsy checkout trust while making external traffic from Instagram, Pinterest, or Google land somewhere more professional.

## Daily outreach target

Start small and consistent:

- 20 shops researched
- 10 messages sent
- 3 follow-ups scheduled
- 1–2 free audits completed
- 1 paid refresh offer made

## First 7-day plan

Day 1:

- Select 20 Etsy shops
- Score them with the 5-point audit structure
- Send 10 soft audit messages

Day 2:

- Send 10 more messages
- Prepare 2 sample audits using screenshots/observations

Day 3:

- Follow up with Day 1 sellers
- Publish one short VitrinY post or Pinterest pin about Etsy listing visuals

Day 4:

- Follow up with Day 2 sellers
- Offer $99 Listing Refresh to warm leads

Day 5:

- Build one sample before/after visual concept for a generic product category

Day 6:

- Send 10 more messages
- Refine niche based on response quality

Day 7:

- Review results
- Keep the best-performing niche
- Raise focus on the offer that got replies

## Key rule

Do not start by selling a full website.

Start by finding a visible pain:

- weak cover image
- inconsistent mockups
- unclear product context
- no brand landing page
- poor social traffic destination

Then offer a small fix.

Small fix first. Bigger system later.

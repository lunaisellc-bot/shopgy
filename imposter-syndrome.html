The "Imposter Syndrome" of Selling Your First Product

Why you feel like a fraud, and how to fix it.

You've designed the product. You've set up the shop. But when it's time to hit "Publish," you freeze. A voice in your head says: "Who are you to charge $50 for this? Everyone else is doing it better."

The "Expert" Trap

We often think we need to be the world's leading expert to sell something. This is false. You don't need to be a guru; you just need to be one step ahead of your customer. If you can save them time or make them feel good, you have earned your price.

"Money is simply a certificate of appreciation for value delivered."

Reframing the Sale

Imposter syndrome comes from focusing on yourself ("Do I look good?"). The cure is focusing on the customer ("Does this help them?").

When you hesitate to sell, you are actually being selfish. You are withholding a solution that someone might desperately need because you are afraid of how you might look.

The 3-Step Mindset Shift

  1. Detach Worth from Price: The price tag is not a measure of your soul's value. It's simply the cost of logistics + profit.
  2. collect "No"s: Make it a game to get rejected. Every "no" brings you closer to the data you need to improve.
  3. Start Small: You don't need to launch a luxury empire on Day 1. Sell one thing to one person. Break the seal.
